# Product

## Register

brand

The marketing site (`/`) and public portfolio pages (`/u/<handle>`) are the brand
surface — the design IS the pitch. The in-app builder (the Expo app) is the product
surface and lives elsewhere.

## Users

Job-seeking software engineers, designers, new grads, and career switchers who need
a credible, shareable place for their work — fast, without fighting a website builder.
They arrive skeptical (the internet is full of AI-generated portfolio tools) and
decide in seconds whether this one is worth installing.

## Product Purpose

Folio builds a live portfolio in a native mobile app, lets AI polish every word and
tailor the CV to any job (using only the user's real experience), and publishes a fast
public page plus Modern/Harvard PDF résumés. The site's job: prove the app is real,
beautiful, and trustworthy, and get the install.

## Brand Personality

Crafted · confident · quietly technical. The voice of an engineer who has taste — it
shows the product working rather than describing it, and never oversells. Three words:
**precise, premium, honest.**

## Anti-references

- Generic AI-SaaS landing pages: cream/sand backgrounds, gradient-text headlines,
  six identical icon+title+body feature cards, a hero-metric stat band, an uppercase
  tracked eyebrow above every section.
- Glowing buttons, gradient background blobs, glassmorphism panels (the user rejected
  these explicitly on the app).
- Fake CSS "product" mockups standing in for real screenshots.

## Design Principles

1. **Show the real product.** Authentic app screenshots in honest device frames beat
   any illustrated abstraction. The site is a demo, not a description.
2. **The gradient is voice, not decoration.** The accent gradient (cobalt → indigo →
   mint) appears on the mark, primary actions, and product imagery — never as a page
   background blob and never inside text.
3. **Vary the cadence.** No repeated section grammar. Each section earns a distinct
   composition; motion fits what it reveals.
4. **Dark, calm, fast.** Near-black surfaces, hairline structure, generous space.
   Restraint with intent — not emptiness.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA contrast on all text (body ≥ 4.5:1 on near-black). Full keyboard paths and
visible focus. Every animation has a `prefers-reduced-motion` fallback. Content is
server-rendered and present in the DOM regardless of JS (reveals only enhance).
